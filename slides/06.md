## Demo 1 — Privacy Policy vs. Actual Product

Duration: ~6 min. Format: Pre-recorded video.

**Setup (30 sec):**
"On the left: your privacy policy. The thing your users click 'I agree' on. On the right: what your product actually does — your analytics setup, your vendor list, your data flows. When was the last time someone checked whether these two sides actually match?"

**The prompt (15 sec):**
Type: "Read our published privacy policy. Then read our analytics configuration, vendor list, and data flow documentation. Find every place where what we promise users doesn't match what we actually do."

**The action (10 sec):**
Claude reads the project folder directly. Show it reading files one by one.
"It's comparing what you say... to what you do."

**The payoff (3 min):**

Finding 1:
"Your privacy policy says: 'We do not share personal data with third-party analytics providers.' Your analytics config shows Mixpanel is active on every page of your patient portal."

Finding 2:
"SendGrid handles patient appointment reminders — patient names and dates. No BAA on file. Your policy says all data processors have signed BAAs."

Finding 3:
"Policy says 30-day log retention. Logging config has no retention limit. Keeping everything forever."

**The land (30 sec):**
"Every one of these is an audit finding waiting to happen. The gaps come from the fact that nobody sees both sides at the same time. The lawyer never saw the analytics dashboard. The engineer never read the policy. Claude Code sees both — because it reads your actual project."

- This demo is about FEAR → RELIEF
- Don't rush the findings — let each one land
- Audience should think "is mine like this?"
- Claude Code differentiator: reads codebase + legal docs simultaneously, not just uploaded files
