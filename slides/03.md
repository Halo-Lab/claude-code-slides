---notes---

"There are now three ways to use Claude. Let me walk you through them so you know what we're working with today."

**Claude AI:**
"This is what most of you use. Claude.ai — the chat. You open it, you type a question, you get an answer. It's great for drafting emails, brainstorming, summarizing a document. Zero setup, works right away. But — and this is important — it has no access to your computer. No files, no folders, no memory between sessions. You copy-paste something in, you get something back. That's it."

**Claude Cowork:**
"This is newer — a desktop app designed for non-developers. It can organize and rename files across folders, extract data from PDFs into spreadsheets, automate copy-paste workflows between apps. No coding needed. It's still in beta, so less control, but if your work is mostly file management and repetitive tasks — this is worth knowing about."

**Claude Code:**
"And this is what I'm showing you today. Don't let the name fool you. Yes, it can write code, but that's not why we're here. Think of it as an autonomous assistant that works directly on your computer."

"One: it reads your actual files. Not uploads — your real project folder. All of it. Eleven compliance documents in six formats? It reads them all at once."

"Two: it can execute. You ask it to analyze something — it doesn't just tell you what to do, it actually does it. Compares documents, builds reports, creates files, saves them to your computer."

"Three: it thinks in steps. It doesn't just give you one answer and stop. It does step one, checks the result, decides what to do next, and keeps going until the job is done."

"The trade-off: you need to review what it produces — it's powerful but not infallible. And there are usage costs. But think of it as a junior analyst sitting at your computer who can read everything, do the work, and hand you the result."

- Walk through all three columns — audience needs the full picture
- Order: Claude AI → Cowork → Claude Code — build to the main event
- Explicitly address the name: "Claude Code" sounds like it's for developers — tell them it's not
